Research Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity can help people realise what they can achieve if they practice, practice, practice. It gives people the motivation to know that if they put in the effort they will be able to master a skill. It helps to boast the morale of people who are learning a new skill especially those who are currently failing at it. It also helps to focus the learning on what will help. E.g step away from rote learning and instead focus on activities that make use of exisiting pathways to help learn the new skill.

Discuss how might engage with the principals of neuroplasticity for your own benefit

Neuroplasticity will benefit me hugely in learning new skills and information. The repetition principle will help me learn coding with repeating the skills over and over my brain will become more familiar with them. Also transference will help where learning one skill or language will help learn another computer skill.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity

Practice, practice, practice. I will try to do a bit of study every day to keep my mind focussed on the task and build thicker neural pathways. Setting goals will help increase my neuroplasticity. Also getting good rest in when I can (a bit of a challenge with two small kids). And also practicing mindfulness.

Research Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant

When people believe they can achieve something they are more likely to be able to achieve it. This is relevant for learning new skills and also relevant for me in this course as if I have this mindset then I will be more likely to handle harder challenges better and be more likely to succeed.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

I feel like I was aware of this before the exploration. However, it has made me think more about it in relation to doing this course. It has highlighted the fact that having this mindset will help me in this course and has made me mindful that when I doubt myself if a challenge is hard I should try to change my mindset to believe that I will be able to achieve it.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

I will believe that I will get to the point where I will be able to computer programme and this will help me reach the goal. When I reach a task that I am struggling with I will try not to doubt my ability to achieve the result but acknowledge that I will get there in the end. I will also change my thinking from "I cant do it", to "I cant do it, yet"
