What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

1. That often my first response is that I cant do something and that I need to change this.
  2. That I need to take some time out in busy life and spend it reflecting. 
3. That I work best when I have a set plan to follow.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

How important it is in the learning process. How the more you understand about yourself and the core learning the easier it is to learn the technical stuff. 

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

The most challenging part was trying to prioritise the core learning part. I would often get more interested and excited about the technical skills as they seems more "important" but when I actually looked into the core skills I realised how important they were as well.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

To make us more employable to prospective employers. Also to help us learn better by understanding how we learn best. 

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practicing programming instead? Justify your answer.

I did struggle at the start to understand the need for so much of it but I do think it is important to touch on this stuff to help us learn better in the future.
